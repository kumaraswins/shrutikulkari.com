The Philosophy

Creating the right conditions for honest emergence.

I've been coaching for over a decade. What keeps me here is the same principle that kept me making art: the deep belief that something profound and honest can emerge when you provide the right space and conditions.

My practice is grounded in strict ICF ethics and disciplined, continuous education. The title of "coach" carries an immense responsibility—one I do not take lightly. Earning the PCC credential wasn't the destination; it is the absolute baseline of excellence I hold myself to.

I partner with executives, creatives, and teams navigating complex transitions, evolving identities, and those quieter, difficult questions that reside underneath the big ones.

Common Questions

Understanding the process.

Clarity is the first step. Here is what you can expect when we decide to partner together.

Executive coaching is a collaborative partnership designed to maximize your personal and professional potential. Unlike therapy (which focuses on healing the past) or consulting (which provides specific answers), coaching provides a thought-provoking process that empowers you to discover your own solutions and strategies for the future.

We meet virtually for 60 minutes, typically bi-weekly. You bring the topic—a challenge, an opportunity, or a feeling of stuckness. Through active listening, powerful questioning, and occasionally creative exercises, we explore the landscape of the issue to generate new awareness and actionable momentum.

I partner with leaders, founders, and senior creatives who are navigating growth, facing complex transitions, or seeking deeper alignment between their professional output and personal values. My clients are typically high-performers looking for an elevated space to reflect.

It is an integrated approach where we use creative metaphors, visual mapping, or expressive exercises to bypass intellectual roadblocks. You do not need to be an artist. It simply allows us to access intuition and insights when traditional analytical conversation hits a wall.
